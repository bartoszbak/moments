# Privacy Policy for Moments

Effective date: April 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how [YOUR FULL NAME] ("I", "me", or "my") handles personal information when you use the Moments mobile application, any widget extensions for the app, and any website or page where this Privacy Policy is posted.

Moments is a personal countdown, reflection, manifestation, and widget app. The app lets users create moments, optionally add photos, optionally sync certain dated moments to Apple Calendar, optionally enable reminder notifications for manifestations, and optionally use AI-powered reflection or manifestation features.

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or want to exercise privacy rights, contact me at [PRIVACY EMAIL ADDRESS].

## 1. Quick Summary

- I do not require you to create an account to use Moments.
- Most of your app content is stored locally on your device and in the app's local storage containers.
- If you use AI features, the text you submit and the generated response are sent to third-party AI service providers I use to power those features.
- If you use subscriptions, purchase-related information is processed by Apple and RevenueCat.
- If you enable calendar sync, the app creates, updates, and deletes calendar events in the calendar you choose.
- If you enable manifestation reminders, the app schedules local notifications on your device.
- I do not sell your personal information and I do not use the app for cross-context behavioral advertising.

## 2. Information We Collect

We collect or process the following categories of information, depending on how you use the app.

### A. Information you create in the app

This may include:

- moment titles
- optional descriptions or notes
- target dates
- whether a moment is a future manifestation
- selected colors, symbols, icon preferences, widget preferences, and appearance settings
- AI-generated reflection or manifestation text saved to the moment
- reminder preferences such as enabled state, reminder rhythm, time, and weekday
- a locally stored identifier for any calendar event created by the app

This information is primarily stored locally on your device using the app's local database and storage.

### B. Photos you choose

If you choose a photo background for a moment, the app requests access to your photo library so you can select an image. The selected image is then stored locally for use in the app and widget.

We do not use photo backgrounds for the app's AI requests based on the current version of the app.

### C. Widget data shared on your device

If you use the widget, the app stores a subset of moment data in an Apple App Group container so the widget extension can display it. This may include:

- title
- date
- created date
- style choices such as colors and symbol
- widget display preferences
- a locally stored thumbnail path for widget images

This sharing happens locally on your device between the app and its widget extension.

### D. Calendar-related data

If you enable calendar sync, the app requests calendar permission and may:

- read the list of writable calendars available on your device
- let you select a calendar
- create, update, and delete all-day events for eligible moments
- store the identifier of calendar events it created so they can be updated or removed later

The app is designed to use calendar access only for the sync features you enable. However, Apple requires broad calendar permission for these features.

### E. Notification-related data

If you enable manifestation reminders, the app requests notification permission and may:

- read your notification authorization status
- schedule recurring local notifications on your device
- include your moment title and, in some cases, saved guidance text in the notification content

### F. Subscription and purchase data

If you view or purchase Moments Plus, purchase-related information is processed by Apple and RevenueCat. I may receive or process information such as:

- subscription status
- purchased product or entitlement status
- anonymous app user identifiers generated by RevenueCat
- offering/package availability
- transaction and restore status

The app does not collect your full payment card number. Payments are handled by Apple.

### G. AI feature request data

If you use AI features, the app sends relevant text inputs to our AI service provider stack. Based on the current implementation, this may include:

- the moment title
- the moment description, if provided
- the moment date or date context
- whether the moment is past, upcoming, or a future manifestation
- date-relative context generated by the app, such as "days until" or "days since"

The generated AI output is returned to the app and may be stored with the moment on your device.

### H. Technical and configuration data

Third-party service providers and platform providers may also receive limited technical or transaction-related metadata, such as:

- device/platform context needed to operate the app or purchase flow
- request metadata for AI calls, such as timestamps, token counts, and latency
- app configuration and offering status data for subscriptions

## 4. When We Share Information

I do not sell your personal information. I do not share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

I may share information only in the following limited ways:

### A. With service providers that operate app features for me

#### OpenRouter and upstream AI model providers

When you use AI features, relevant prompt content and generated responses are transmitted to OpenRouter and may be routed by OpenRouter to the model provider used to generate the response. Those providers may process the content under their own terms and privacy policies.

This means that when you use AI features, the text you submit for AI generation should be treated as data shared with third-party AI providers.

#### RevenueCat

I use RevenueCat to manage subscription offerings, entitlement status, purchase state, and restore flows.

#### Apple

Apple may process information when you use:

- App Store purchases and subscriptions
- Calendar access and calendar event creation
- notification delivery
- the photo picker
- widgets and app extension functionality

### B. For legal or safety reasons

I may disclose information if required to do so by law, subpoena, court order, or other valid legal process, or if I believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, or property.

### C. In a business transfer

If I am involved in a sale of the app, transfer of assets, restructuring, bankruptcy, or a similar transaction, information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to applicable law.

## 6. Data Retention

I retain information for as long as needed for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

In practice:

- app content is generally retained locally on your device until you edit or delete it, clear the app's data, or remove the app
- calendar event identifiers are retained locally for as long as needed to maintain synced events
- reminder settings are retained locally until you change them or remove the app
- subscription status data may be refreshed from RevenueCat while the app is installed and used
- third-party providers may retain data according to their own retention practices and legal obligations

Because much of Moments is device-local, deleting moments or removing the app may remove a significant portion of the locally stored data, but this will not automatically delete information already processed by Apple, RevenueCat, OpenRouter, or other providers.
